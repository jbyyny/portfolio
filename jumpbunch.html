JumpBunch is a site targeted for parents and guardians of young children. JumpBunch is a year-round fitness program for ages 2-12. This Silicon Valley franchise site was designed and built as the new design template for all U.S. JumpBunch franchises.

Goal: Design and develop a responsive website targeted to tech-savvy Silicon Valley parents and guardians. Inform users of this kids fitness program, enroll and convert to members. Features offered included: sign up, class schedules and registration, tuition payment, personal photo galleries and apply to become a coach.

Team: 2 engineers, 1 product lead, 1 product designer


Process

  • Conducted competitive audit to help understand competition and target audience site map to denote inventory of content and allow for scalability to define phased approach of features
  • Sketched flows for each user type to begin mapping UX
  • Created architecture of system
  • Wireframed basic pages to map content, identify actions available by user roles and to plan flows
  • Defined design language and determined assets (photos and forms) needed
  • Explored mobile designs in parallel with desktop experience
  • Continued with several rounds of design iterations and refinement
  • Provided specs for engineers
  • Provided design QE feedback


Project Results

  • Parallax site design and experience for sophisticated Silicon Valley audience
  • Responsive design
  • Completed flows for online registration and payment
  • Launched Alpha in January 2015
